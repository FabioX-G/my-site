Basics of Cell, DNA, & Gene

On average, the human body has around 40 to 60 trillion cells:

  • These cells are the basic units of life, containing chromosomes in their nucleus.

  • Chromosomes are made of DNA, which is organized into genes. Each DNA is made up of three main components: a sugar molecule, a phosphate molecule, and a nucleobase.

  • Each gene contains instructions for making a specific protein, and these instructions are coded by the order of nucleobases (A, T, C, and G) within the DNA molecule.

What are cancer cells? What causes cancer?

Cancer cells are abnormal cells that grow and divide uncontrollably in the body. They develop when the DNA within a cell becomes damaged or mutated, leading to errors in the instructions for cell growth and division.

Cancer can arise from almost any type of cell in the body and can affect people of all ages. The exact cause of cancer is often not known, but it is thought to involve a combination of genetic, environmental, and lifestyle factors. [2]

What are the traditional ways of killing cancer?

  • Surgery: A major drawback of surgery is that it may not remove all cancer cells since cancer cells can multiply indefinitely and travel throughout the body via the bloodstream or immune system. [2]

  • Chemotherapy & Radiation Therapy: Although chemotherapy and radiation therapy may kill cancer cells, they can also damage healthy cells, which can cause potential health risks. [2]

So what are some new ways of killing cancer?

  • Antiangiogenesis: This treatment works by inhibiting the formation of new blood vessels, cutting off the supply of nutrients and oxygen to cancer cells, thus slowing or stopping tumor growth. [3]

  • Nanoparticles: These tiny particles can deliver cancer-fighting drugs directly to tumor cells, increasing the effectiveness of treatment while minimizing damage to healthy cells. [3]

  • New drugs: Innovative drugs can target specific cancer-causing genes or proteins, disrupting the growth and spread of cancer cells more precisely and with fewer side effects. [3]

  • Gene therapy: By correcting or replacing faulty genes, this approach aims to fix the underlying genetic abnormalities that drive cancer growth and progression. [2]

  • Immunotherapy: This treatment harnesses the power of the patient's immune system to recognize, attack, and eliminate cancer cells more effectively, boosting the body's natural defenses against the disease [2]. Imagine turning your body's own defense system into a cancer-crushing powerhouse!
